## 1. Event Overview

### Concept

Base Camp Kids is a one-day camping-themed Children's Day activation by Camping Nigeria. It transforms a safe outdoor field into a bustling kid's camp with tents, house teams, creative stations, outdoor games, kid-friendly food, and keepsake souvenirs. The tone is bubbly, safe, and well-organized, staying true to the Camping Nigeria promise: Adventure made simple.

The event is deliberately designed to look like a scaled-down version of our Multi-day On-Campus Camps signature product. That means every family who attends leaves with a clear taste of what our full school programs deliver. Children's Day becomes both a celebration and a low-pressure sales showcase.

### Objectives

- Give children a memorable, fun outdoor experience that feels like a real camp.

- Give parents full confidence in the safety, structure, and professionalism of Camping Nigeria.

- Strengthen our visible brand presence with school heads, PTAs, and enrichment buyers present.

- Generate warm leads and content assets that feed the 90-day GTM pipeline.

- Test our event delivery muscle for future public activations.

### Success Metrics

#### Attendance

- Target headcount booked: 80 children (default working number).

- Show-up rate on the day: 85% or higher.

- Parent-to-child turnout: at least 1 parent or guardian per child.

#### Experience

- Parent satisfaction survey: 4.5 out of 5 average or higher.

- Zero safety incidents requiring medical escalation.

- Every child leaves with a completed souvenir T-shirt and a camper certificate.

#### Business

- 30 or more qualified leads captured (parents, school reps, partners).

- 10 or more school enquiries for our Nature & Craft or On-Campus Camps programs.

- At least 1 hero reel, 3 carousel sets, and a full branded photo gallery delivered within 72 hours.

### Setup Plan (Site Layout)

Think of the site as six zones. All zones are signposted in brand colours so children can navigate independently with their house leader.

- Gate and Registration Zone: entrance tables, wristband station, consent check, parent sign-in sheet.

- Tent Village: 4 pop-up tents in house colours, used for shade, photos, and quiet moments.

- Activity Circuit: 6 to 8 stations laid out in a horseshoe so house groups can rotate cleanly.

- Food and Mocktail Zone: covered area, separate from activities, with waste bins and handwashing station.

- Quiet and First Aid Zone: clearly marked, stocked first aid kit, shaded rest seats, a calm-down space for overstimulated kids.

- Stage and Photo Wall: where opening, closing, certificate ceremony, and the Base Camp selfie spot live.

Power needs: sound system (PA), lighting for tents if the event runs late, extension cables taped down. Confirm generator or mains backup.

Waste management: 4 to 6 bins across the site, clearly labelled. Cleaning crew does a full sweep post-meal and post-event.

### Safety Plan

#### Child safety

- Wristband check-in and check-out with a unique parent code. No child released to anyone whose code does not match.

- Staff-to-child ratios strictly held. House leaders do not leave their group.

- First aid kit stocked, first aider visible in branded vest, emergency numbers printed and posted.

- Allergy list compiled at registration and shared with food team before service.

- All staff briefed on safeguarding basics: no solo adult and child interactions, no unsupervised tent time.

#### Crowd and logistics

- Security at the gate controls entry and exit.

- Car park and drop-off flow agreed with venue.

- Emergency evacuation route walked and shown to all staff before start.

#### Weather backup

- Indoor or covered fallback venue identified and booked. Plan B is triggered if heavy rain is forecast 24 hours before.

- Plan C (on-the-day rain): move all craft and quiet activities under covered tents, condense schedule, keep main meal indoors if available.

### Parent Experience Flow

1.  Arrival: Warm greet, consent form reconfirmed, child wristbanded, parent signed in with pickup code.

2.  Settling: Parent Liaison offers mocktail or water, shows parents to designated parent viewing area, answers questions.

3.  Mid-event: One WhatsApp photo broadcast goes out at 12:30 PM to opted-in parents showing kids in action.

4.  Pickup: Wristband code verified. Child, T-shirt, certificate, and goodie bag handed over.

5.  After: Thank-you message via WhatsApp, link to photo gallery, gentle invitation to our Nature and Craft or On-Campus Camps programs.

### E. Frequently Asked Questions

#### 1. Is the event supervised the whole time?

Yes. Every house group has a dedicated house leader who stays with the group all day. Our staff-to-child ratios are 1 to 8 for ages 6 to 8 and 1 to 10 for ages 9 to 12.

#### 2. What happens if my child has an allergy?

Tell us at registration. We compile a full allergy list and share it with our food team before service. Nut-free trail mix and alternative snacks are always available.

#### 3. What if it rains?

We have a covered fallback plan. If heavy rain is forecast 24 hours before, we will move to the backup venue and notify all parents by WhatsApp. If rain comes on the day, we move activities under our tents and keep the meal and quiet zones operating.

#### 4. Can my child come with friends from school?

Absolutely. Just register them both and note that they would like to be in the same house.

#### 5. Who hands my child over at pickup?

Only an adult with a matching pickup code (the other half of your child's wristband). If someone different will collect your child, message us in advance so we can update the record.

#### 6. Is there a first aider on site?

Yes. A trained first aider is on site the entire time in a clearly marked vest. We maintain a stocked first aid kit and a clear emergency protocol.

#### 7. What food will be served?

A small chops spread (samosa, spring roll, puff puff, meat pie, and more) plus a main meal of jollof rice, grilled chicken, and plantain. A mocktail bar operates all day. Vegetarian and allergy-safe options are prepared when flagged.

#### 8. Can parents stay?

You are welcome in the parent viewing area. Activity stations themselves are child-only, which is what keeps the camp feel intact and lets children fully immerse.

#### 9. Will my child appear on social media?

Only if you tick yes on the consent form. Children whose parents tick no will wear a visible blue wristband, and our photographer will not include them in posted content.

#### 10. What does my child take home?

A camping-themed T-shirt (decorated by them), a camp certificate, a goodie bag, and in many cases, new friends.

#### 11. Can I get a refund?

Full refund if cancelled more than 7 days out. 50% refund if cancelled within 7 days. No refund within 48 hours of the event.

#### 12. What if my child is anxious about the camp idea?

This is a daytime event with parents able to stay nearby. Our house leaders are trained to welcome shy children gently. We have a Quiet Zone with books and cushions for children who need a break.
