**Program Overview**

**Nature & Craft** is a single-day immersive experience that blends
outdoor adventure, creative expression, and environmental education.
Designed for schools, campuses, and youth groups, the program helps
participants build a deeper relationship with nature through hands-on
activities, cultural art practices, teamwork, and reflection.

Delivered either **on campus or off campus**, each experience runs for
**4 to 8 hours** and can be customized by age group, learning goals,
location, and group size.

**Core Program Idea**

Nature & Craft brings together:

- **Eco-awareness learning**

- **Outdoor activities**

- **Artistic and cultural experiences**

- **Team bonding and recreation**

- **Hands-on environmental stewardship**

The goal is not only to teach environmental responsibility, but to make
it memorable through movement, making, storytelling, and shared
experience.

**Program Objectives**

Participants will:

- Understand basic environmental concepts in an engaging, practical way

- Build appreciation for nature, culture, and community

- Develop teamwork, problem-solving, and communication skills

- Explore creativity through traditional and nature-inspired art

- Practice stewardship through action-based eco activities

- Experience outdoor recreation in a safe, structured setting

**Suggested Program Modules**

You mentioned adire, camping setup, bonfire, sporting events, and
immersive outdoor activity. Here is a strong modular structure:

**Module 1: Eco-Awareness Session**

A curriculum-aligned introduction to the environment, sustainability,
and stewardship.

Topics can include:

- Understanding our environment

- Waste and pollution

- Biodiversity and habitats

- Climate awareness

- Water, land, and responsible resource use

- Everyday environmental action

**Outcome:** Participants leave with practical environmental
understanding and clear action points.

**Module 2: Nature Discovery Activity**

A guided outdoor experience that helps learners observe and connect with
their surroundings.

Activities:

- Guided nature walk

- Observation journals

- Texture, color, and sound mapping

- Nature scavenger hunt

- Local ecosystem exploration

**Outcome:** Participants become more observant, curious, and connected
to place.

**Module 3: Adire and Creative Heritage Workshop**

A hands-on artistic session led in collaboration with Ngozi Akande Arts.

Activities:

- Introduction to adire and textile traditions

- Symbol, pattern, and storytelling through fabric

- Tie and dye practical session

- Nature-inspired color and motif exploration

- Take-home art piece

**Outcome:** Participants engage with Nigerian cultural heritage while
expressing creativity.

**Module 4: Camping Experience**

A practical and exciting introduction to outdoor living.

Activities:

- Tent setup demonstration

- Team-based camp setup challenge

- Camp layout basics

- Outdoor safety and equipment introduction

- Camp etiquette and shared responsibility

**Outcome:** Participants learn confidence, teamwork, and outdoor
readiness.

**Module 5: Sports and Team Recreation**

High-energy outdoor play that strengthens teamwork and joy.

Activities:

- Sack races

- Relay competitions

- Tug of war

- Football mini-games

- Cooperative challenges

- Fitness or movement games

**Outcome:** Participants develop teamwork, resilience, and physical
engagement.

**Module 6: Bonfire and Reflection Experience**

Best suited to off-campus or approved outdoor venues.

Activities:

- Bonfire circle

- Storytelling

- Music, drumming, or spoken word

- Reflection prompts

- Group sharing on environmental responsibility

- Closing ceremony

**Outcome:** The experience ends with emotional connection,
memory-making, and meaning.
