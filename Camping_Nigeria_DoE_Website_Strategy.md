PART A: STRATEGY

# A1.  Naming and Positioning

## Why We Lead With Duke of Edinburgh

Every school in Nigeria that runs this program uses the name Duke of Edinburgh on their own website. That is the term principals know, parents Google, and students talk about. Leading with the International Award for Young People - its official name in Nigeria - would cause unnecessary confusion at the top of the page.

The correct approach: lead with Duke of Edinburgh in headlines and callouts because that is the recognition trigger, then clarify in body copy that it is officially administered in Nigeria as the International Award for Young People. This reads as knowledgeable rather than loose, which builds trust with Award Coordinators who know the difference.

Important: Camping Nigeria is not an accredited Award operator and should never be presented as one. The page positions us as the outdoor delivery partner - the company that handles the camping, hiking, and outdoor programming that schools need to execute the expedition component. Award administration sits with the school's Licensed Operator.

# A2.  Page Architecture

## /schools Page: Where the Callout Lives

The callout sits between the Why Outdoor Learning Matters section and the Our School Programs section. This gives it flagship status without removing the existing programs. # A3.  The Assessment: Logic and Design

## Why an Assessment, Not a Form

A contact form asks for information. An assessment gives something back. The person finishes it knowing which option fits their situation, which means they arrive at the booking call already oriented. You arrive knowing who they are before you say hello.

## Who Takes It

- A principal or teacher who wants to understand whether Camping Nigeria is right for their school

- A parent whose child is enrolled in an Award program and wants to know what the expedition involves

## How It Works

- Four questions, one at a time, on-screen

- Takes under ninety seconds to complete

- Results screen immediately: their recommended tier, what it includes, and two options - book a call now or get a proposal by email

- Name, email, and school or organisation name collected before or alongside the results screen

Lead capture timing: collect name and email before showing results. This ensures you have the contact even if they close the tab after seeing their recommendation. The results screen should still feel rewarding, not like a gate.

The Duke of Edinburgh Award - officially known in Nigeria as the International Award for Young People - is a globally recognised program that challenges young people across four areas: physical activity, skill development, voluntary service, and an outdoor expedition.

The Award is administered in Nigeria by the International Award Association Nigeria. To learn more about how the program works and how schools get accredited, visit the links below.

## Section 3: The Expedition Component

SECTION LABEL: THE EXPEDITION

What the Expedition Actually Involves

The expedition is the outdoor component of the Award. Students plan and carry out a journey in a natural environment - usually over one to four days depending on the Award level. They camp overnight, navigate their route, and complete the journey as a team.

For Bronze level, this is typically a two-day, one-night expedition. Silver and Gold levels involve longer journeys with higher demands.

Schools are responsible for ensuring students are prepared and that the expedition meets Award requirements. That preparation involves planning routes, sourcing equipment, arranging facilitation, and making sure the outdoor experience is safe and structured.

Most schools find this the hardest part to execute well. That is where we come in.

SECTION LABEL: COMMON QUESTIONS

Frequently Asked Questions

Are you an accredited Duke of Edinburgh or International Award operator?

No. We are an outdoor programming and equipment partner. We support schools in delivering the camping and outdoor activity components of the expedition. Your Award Coordinator or Licensed Operator confirms compliance with the Award framework.

Can you work with our school even if we are not yet running the Award?

Yes. We work with schools running the Award and schools that run outdoor enrichment programs, end-of-term camps, and leadership experiences. The expedition support is one part of what we do.

What if we already have some of our own equipment?

We can provide facilitation and programming without the full equipment package. Tell us what you already have and we will build a quote around your actual needs.

How many students can you accommodate?

Our standard capacity is up to 100 students. Larger groups can be discussed and may require additional facilitator teams.

How far in advance do we need to book?

We recommend six to eight weeks for full program packages and two to four weeks for equipment rental only. Booking early gives you first pick of available dates.

# C2.  Assessment Technical Notes

- The assessment can be built in Typeform, Tally, or a custom component depending on your tech stack.

- Lead data (name, email, school name) must be captured to a CRM or email list before the results screen renders.

- The results screen should display inline - no redirect to a new page. The calendar embed appears directly below the results copy.

- If the user selects D on Question 4 (not sure yet), default to Trail Ready as the recommendation. This is the mid-tier and gives your team the most useful starting point for the call.

- If the user selects D on Question 1 (exploring, not sure), the results copy should soften slightly - use the same tier recommendation but open with: 'Based on what you have shared, here is where most schools in your position start.'

- Store all assessment responses alongside the lead data. This tells your team who they are before the call begins.
